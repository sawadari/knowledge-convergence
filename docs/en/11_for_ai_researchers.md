# For AI Researchers

Knowledge Convergence is relevant to AI research because it addresses the gap between model output and accountable use.

Many AI evaluations focus on whether an output is correct, helpful, safe, or preferred. Knowledge Convergence asks an additional question:

> Can this output enter a responsible decision and execution process?

## Research themes

Knowledge Convergence provides research opportunities in:

1. decision-ready knowledge states
2. evidence-aware reasoning
3. agent delegation and bounded execution
4. human and organization-aware AI systems
5. validation beyond benchmark accuracy
6. conformance checking for AI-mediated workflows
7. multi-agent governance
8. hold, reopen, rollback, and escalation as first-class outcomes
9. measurement of evidence coverage and domain validity
10. evaluation of meaningful human oversight

## Output quality is not enough

An AI output may be fluent and plausible, but still unusable because:

- evidence is missing
- context is unclear
- responsibility is undefined
- validation is missing
- tool authority is not granted
- rollback is impossible
- the human reviewer cannot meaningfully intervene

This creates research questions beyond text quality.

## Epistemic vs governance uncertainty

AI systems often mix two different problems:

- Is the claim likely to be true?
- Is the organization allowed to act on it?

Knowledge Convergence separates these through epistemic convergence and governance convergence.

## Agent governance

Agentic systems require new evaluation targets:

- authority envelope correctness
- tool scope compliance
- audit completeness
- stop condition detection
- rollback readiness
- escalation correctness
- post-action validation

## Possible research questions

- How can evidence coverage be measured without overclaiming certainty?
- How should authority envelopes be represented for AI agents?
- How can a system distinguish confidence from authorization?
- How can valid hold states be evaluated?
- How can human oversight be tested for meaningfulness?
- How should multi-agent systems prevent responsibility diffusion?
- How can domain validity be evaluated across different engineering domains?

## Relation to Systems Engineering

Systems Engineering provides a strong domain for studying Knowledge Convergence because SE naturally involves stakeholder needs, requirements, trade-offs, verification, validation, human roles, and organizational accountability.
